Australian Government Initiative

Solar battery storage just got more affordable.

The federal Cheaper Home Batteries Program provides a tiered rebate on eligible solar battery installations: $244/kWh on the first 14 kWh of usable capacity, $146/kWh from 14 to 28 kWh, and $37/kWh from 28 to 50 kWh. For the 10.24 kWh PowerBox Pro, that is a rebate of approximately $2,500, with larger systems eligible for up to $7,204.

Yes. The federal Cheaper Home Batteries Program provides a tiered rebate on solar battery installations: $244/kWh on the first 14 kWh of usable capacity, $146/kWh from 14 to 28 kWh, and $37/kWh from 28 to 50 kWh. In practical terms: a 10 kWh solar battery attracts around $2,440; a 20 kWh system around $4,438; a 30 kWh system around $6,463; and systems of 50 kWh or more receive the maximum rebate of $7,204. As feed-in tariffs continue to fall, a well-sized solar battery ensures the energy your panels generate stays in your home rather than being exported for a fraction of what you pay to import it. We track every available incentive and include all applicable savings in your proposal automatically.

Battery sizing should match your home's nighttime energy consumption. For most Australian homes, a solar battery in the 10–16 kWh range covers overnight usage comfortably while leaving a reserve for blackout protection. If you're on a time-of-use tariff or want to trade energy back to the grid, a larger system of 16–20 kWh may deliver better returns. We size every solar battery system to your specific usage profile during your free consultation.

LiFePO4 (lithium iron phosphate) is the gold standard chemistry for home solar batteries. Compared to older lithium-ion chemistries, LiFePO4 batteries are significantly safer (no thermal runaway risk), deliver 6,000+ charge cycles at 90% depth of discharge, and maintain usable capacity for 14–16 years in Australian conditions. It's why leading solar battery manufacturers, including Dyness, use LiFePO4 exclusively for residential energy storage.

Yes, when correctly configured for backup operation. The Solis Hybrid S6 inverter switches to battery power in under 4 milliseconds during a grid outage, fast enough that most appliances don't even notice the transition. Your solar panels also continue charging the battery during a blackout, so as long as there's sunlight, your energy supply keeps replenishing. We configure backup circuits during installation to protect your essential loads: lights, refrigeration, and other critical devices.
